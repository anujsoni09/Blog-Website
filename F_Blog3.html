Why Fitness is More Important Than Dieting

Written by: Betty Jensen (updated on April 25, 2021)




It doesn’t matter if you are getting fit or trying to lose weight, everyone should be striving for health and fitness. In addition to this, health and fitness are both essential components in a sound lifestyle. Regardless of your age, health and fitness have a way of being treated as more important than diet and exercise.

Fitness and health are inseparable. If one is lacking, the other will be affected. Physical fitness and health are interrelated in that fitness promotes health while health promotes fitness. Physical health refers to what happens to your body and more specifically, how your body functions. The term encompasses the effects that lifestyle, environmental, and genetic factors have on your overall health. Mental and emotional health, if you have a good frame of mind, also has an effect on your physical health. Your posture, blood pressure, heart rate, breathing, blood sugar, and digestive system are all linked to the emotional and mental state you are in.

Since so much of our life is connected to our body’s sense of well-being, it makes sense that fitness and health are interrelated. If you are in poor physical shape, you will have trouble keeping yourself emotionally stable.
Fitness also helps with the daily functioning of your body. It reduces the risk of diseases, such as heart disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, osteoporosis, stroke, and cancer. This is important because it means your body is functioning at peak efficiency.

In addition to helping you feel better physically, physical activity can help you feel better mentally. You may take part in aerobics classes to lose weight, but anaerobic exercises such as running, bicycling, or swimming can improve your mood and your health. In fact, aerobic exercise not only helps you burn fat and increase energy, it also helps you improve your memory, focus, and concentration.
There are various studies that point to the connection between fitness and health. For example, in one study conducted by the University of Michigan, participants were asked to fill out questionnaires regarding their perceived levels of energy and vitality. Those who said they were unfit rated themselves as having less energy and vitality than those who rated themselves as being fit.

Other studies have linked fitness to environmental factors such as depression, anxiety, and physical stress. If you suffer from these conditions, then you can benefit from physical activity, and if you have never had a history of depression, anxiety, or stress, then you might be better off working on your fitness than taking prescription drugs.
And, of course, our mental health affects our physical health in ways we can’t even imagine. If you are overweight, you are more likely to develop heart disease. Depression increases the risk of high blood pressure, which in turn can cause more heart problems, which in turn can cause more weight gain. How can you be sure you are in good health? Simple – get regular checkups, and always make sure your doctor tells you what kind of health you are in.

Physical fitness is a great way to become healthier. If you do exercise regularly, you can prevent yourself from developing conditions like obesity, heart disease, diabetes, and arthritis. You also reduce your chances of developing anxiety, depression, and stress.
Proper nutrition and exercise together are very powerful tools in your arsenal. You don’t have to go on an epic detox diet to start feeling better, but you can help your body to function better through better nutrition and physical activity. There is no time like the present to start living healthier and getting more fit.
